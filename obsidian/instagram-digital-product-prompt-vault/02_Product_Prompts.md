# Product Prompts

## 1. Digital Product Idea Generator

```text
I want to create a digital product to sell on Instagram.

Use the following inputs:
- Niche:
- My skills / experience:
- Target audience:
- Their current situation:
- Their biggest problem:
- Desired transformation:
- How fast they want results:
- Price sensitivity:
- What format I can realistically create fastest:
- Existing assets I already have (posts, voice notes, templates, frameworks, examples):
- My preferred product type, if any:

Generate the top 5 most commercially viable digital product ideas, ranked from strongest to weakest using this scoring logic:
- ease of creation
- speed to market
- clarity of result
- likelihood of impulse purchase
- upsell potential
- suitability for Instagram content-led selling

For each idea, provide:
1. Product name
2. One-line promise
3. Core buyer problem it solves
4. Why this product will sell to this audience
5. Best format (ebook, template, mini-course, toolkit, checklist, swipe file, audit, planner, calculator, script pack, etc.)
6. What to include inside
7. Suggested price range
8. Why that price makes sense
9. Estimated time to create
10. Best buyer type for this product
11. Fastest version I can launch as an MVP in 48 hours
12. Natural upsell or next offer

Then finish with:
- Best first product to launch now
- Why it beats the others
- Biggest risk
- How to reduce that risk

Do not give generic advice. Make it commercially sharp, realistic, and specific.
```

## 2. Product Outline Builder

```text
I want to create a digital product.

Use these inputs:
- Product type:
- Topic:
- Target audience:
- Starting pain/problem:
- Desired transformation:
- Price point:
- Delivery format:
- Buyer sophistication level (cold / warm / problem-aware / solution-aware):
- My proof assets or lived experience:
- My tone / brand voice:

Build a complete premium product outline.

Output:
1. Product name
2. Subtitle that clearly explains the transformation
3. One-sentence positioning statement
4. Who it is for
5. Who it is not for
6. Full table of contents with 5-7 sections
7. For each section, include:
   - section objective
   - 3-5 things the buyer will learn
   - one practical action item
   - one mistake to avoid
8. The fastest quick win the buyer can get in the first 10 minutes
9. Suggested bonus assets
10. Suggested worksheets, templates, or checklists
11. Estimated completion time for the buyer
12. Why this feels worth the price
13. Top 3 objections a buyer may have
14. How the product itself answers those objections
15. A stripped-down MVP version and a fuller premium version

Finish with:
- Best final title
- Best subtitle
- The core promise
- The strongest sales angle

Make it feel premium, outcome-driven, and immediately buildable.
```

## Quick Use Note
Use these when:
- you do not yet have the offer
- the product idea is still fuzzy
- you need an MVP that can be shipped fast
- you want the model to think commercially, not poetically like a caffeinated intern
